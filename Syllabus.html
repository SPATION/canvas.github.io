Prerequisites

This is an introductory level course. No prior experience with conference room technology is assumed. A basic knowledge of the technology will be useful, however, to build on as you select and experience those best suited to your communication goals.

Course Goals and Objectives

By completing this course, you'll be able to:

  • get started with equipment in the conference rooms and employ technology thoughtfully,
  • understand the strengths and limitations of various technological tools and mediums,
  • develop technical skills as well as a vocabulary of standard terms about the running of conference room technology,
  • actively evaluate common issues when taking on equipment,
  • interact with peers who also experience this technology to connect.

Course Calendar and Schedule

You may work at your own pace through the content.

Application-get started with equipment in the conference rooms and employ technology thoughtfully, e.g., to apply from a list, map or, forecast,

Analysis-understand the strengths and limitations of various technological tools and mediums, e.g., to analyze in a questionnaire, argument, or breakdown,

Synthesis-develop technical skills as well as a vocabulary of standard terms about the running of conference room technology, e.g., to synthesize in a hypothesis, an alternative action, or a set of standards,

Evaluation-actively evaluate common issues when taking on equipment, e.g., to evaluate in a survey, an assessment, or a standard established,

Evaluation-interact with peers who also experience this technology to connect, e.g., to evaluate in a discussion, valuing, or a recommendation.
